Quick answers about the English Writing, Rhetoric and Technology degree, online options, careers, certificates, and creative nonfiction opportunities.

Yes—especially when it’s designed for today’s writing-focused careers. The English Writing, Rhetoric and Technology degree at CU Denver emphasizes professional writing, digital communication, rhetoric, creative nonfiction, and AI-supported writing practices. Students graduate with a portfolio of real projects and marketable skills that employers value across industries.

While traditional English degrees often focus primarily on literary analysis, English Writing, Rhetoric and Technology focuses on writing as a professional, civic, creative, and digital practice. Students study rhetoric, linguistics, professional and technical writing, digital media, creative nonfiction, and AI-supported writing.

At the same time, this degree offers a great deal of flexibility. Students can take courses in literature and creative writing alongside their English Writing, Rhetoric and Technology coursework, allowing them to shape the degree around their interests while still gaining practical, career-focused skills.

Yes. The English Writing, Rhetoric and Technology degree can be completed fully online, fully on campus, or through a combination of both. The same CU Denver faculty teach online and on-campus courses, so students receive the same high-quality instruction in every format.

Graduates pursue careers in areas such as:

  • Technical and professional writing
  • UX writing and content strategy
  • Editing, publishing, and creative nonfiction writing
  • Marketing and digital media
  • Nonprofit and public communication
  • Teaching English to speakers of other languages (TESOL)
  • Grant and proposal writing

Because strong writing, storytelling, and communication skills are in demand across fields, the English Writing, Rhetoric and Technology degree prepares students for a wide range of career paths.

Yes. Students can earn stackable undergraduate certificates alongside the English Writing, Rhetoric and Technology degree, including Writing and Editing with AI, Technical and Professional Writing, Teaching English Language Learners (TESOL), Grant and Proposal Writing, and Narrative Nonfiction.

This degree is a strong fit for students who enjoy writing, storytelling, and analysis; want practical career preparation; and are interested in how writing, technology, and AI shape communication today. It’s especially well suited for students comparing online English degrees, professional writing programs, technical writing degrees, or creative nonfiction pathways in the Denver metro area or beyond.

Yes. Courses emphasize project-based learning, and students graduate with a professional portfolio that may include professional documents, digital projects, research-based writing, and creative nonfiction. This portfolio helps students demonstrate their skills to employers and graduate programs.

Absolutely. The degree provides strong preparation for graduate study in writing, rhetoric, creative nonfiction, linguistics, education, TESOL, digital media, and related fields, as well as for professional schools that value advanced communication and writing skills.
