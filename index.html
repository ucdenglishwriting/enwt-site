Frequently Asked Questions

Is an English degree worth it?

Yes—especially when it’s designed for today’s writing-focused careers. The English Writing, Rhetoric and Technology degree at CU Denver emphasizes professional writing, digital communication, rhetoric, creative nonfiction, and AI-supported writing practices. Students graduate with a portfolio of real projects and marketable skills valued across industries.

What is the difference between English Writing, Rhetoric and Technology and a traditional English literature degree?

While traditional English degrees often focus primarily on literary analysis, English Writing, Rhetoric and Technology focuses on writing as a professional, civic, creative, and digital practice. Students study rhetoric, linguistics, professional and technical writing, digital media, creative nonfiction, and AI-supported writing.

At the same time, the degree offers significant flexibility. Students can take courses in literature and creative writing alongside their English Writing, Rhetoric and Technology coursework, allowing them to shape the degree around their interests while still gaining practical, career-focused skills.

Is the English Writing, Rhetoric and Technology degree available online?

Yes. The degree can be completed fully online, fully on campus, or through a combination of both. The same CU Denver faculty teach online and on-campus courses.

What kinds of jobs can I get with this degree?

Graduates pursue careers in technical and professional writing, UX writing and content strategy, editing and publishing, creative nonfiction, marketing and digital media, nonprofit and public communication, TESOL, and grant and proposal writing.

Do students build a portfolio?

Yes. Students graduate with a professional portfolio that may include professional documents, digital projects, research-based writing, and creative nonfiction—an asset for both employers and graduate programs.
